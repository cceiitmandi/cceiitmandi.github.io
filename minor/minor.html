Minor in Data Science

The Minor in Data Science is designed to provide students with foundational knowledge and practical skills in data analysis, statistical modeling, and machine learning. This program equips students with the ability to extract meaningful insights from complex datasets and make data-driven decisions.

This interdisciplinary program combines mathematical foundations, programming skills, and domain-specific knowledge to prepare students for careers in data analytics, business intelligence, and data science roles across various industries.

Through hands-on projects and real-world case studies, students will develop expertise in data visualization, predictive modeling, and machine learning algorithms. The curriculum emphasizes both theoretical understanding and practical application, ensuring graduates are well-prepared for the demands of the modern data-driven economy.

Minor in Cyber Security

The Minor in Cyber Security addresses the growing need for skilled professionals in protecting digital assets and infrastructure. This program covers essential concepts in network security, cryptography, ethical hacking, and risk management.

In an era where cyber threats are increasingly sophisticated, this program provides comprehensive training in identifying vulnerabilities, implementing security protocols, and responding to security incidents. Students will learn about threat analysis, security protocols, incident response, and compliance frameworks.

The curriculum emphasizes both theoretical knowledge and practical skills through lab sessions and case studies. Students will gain hands-on experience with security tools and technologies, preparing them for careers in cybersecurity, information technology, and related fields.

Minor in Artificial Intelligence

The Minor in Artificial Intelligence introduces students to the fundamentals of AI and machine learning. This program covers core concepts including neural networks, natural language processing, computer vision, and robotics.

With AI transforming industries across the globe, this program provides students with the technical foundation to understand and develop intelligent systems. Through coursework and projects, students will explore how machines can perceive, reason, and learn from data.

The curriculum includes programming for AI, machine learning algorithms, deep learning, and AI ethics. Students will develop intelligent systems and applications as part of their coursework, gaining valuable experience that prepares them for careers in AI development, research, and innovation.
